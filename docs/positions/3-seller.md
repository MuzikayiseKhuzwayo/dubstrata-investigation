## 1. Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure (SOP) defines the operational standards and tactical execution steps for the business's critical commercial functions: generating revenue, acquiring new customers, and managing the sales pipeline.

Commercial viability requires predictable, repeatable, and scalable sales processes. Without structured execution, client acquisition becomes erratic, forecasting becomes inaccurate, and resource planning fails. The goal of this document is to ensure that every sales lead, whether inbound or outbound, is handled with consistent professionalism, qualified objectively, guided through a consultative evaluation, closed transparently, and transitioned smoothly to the execution or delivery team. This directly protects revenue predictability, controls the cost of customer acquisition (CAC), and protects the company's brand reputation.

---

## 2. Scope

This procedure applies to all team members executing the "Seller" function, specifically those operating in sales development, account executive, and sales management roles.

* **In-Scope:**
  * Monitoring and triaging inbound leads generated by marketing campaigns.
  * Researching, segmenting, and conducting outbound prospecting.
  * Conducting discovery calls, product/service demonstrations, and evaluations.
  * Negotiating pricing, presenting proposals, and finalizing service agreements.
  * Recording pipeline progression and deal metrics within the Customer Relationship Management (CRM) system.
  * Conducting internal handovers to the Customer Success (CS) or Delivery teams post-signature.
* **Out-of-Scope:**
  * Generating marketing collateral, managing paid ad spend, or organizing webinars (managed under marketing procedures).
  * Creating customized, legally binding Master Services Agreements (MSAs) from scratch without legal review (managed under legal procedures).
  * Post-sale implementation, onboarding configuration, and ongoing product support (managed under Customer Success procedures).
  * Standard price book adjustments and fundamental product discounting policy formulation (managed under leadership and finance procedures).

## 3. Definitions and Acronyms

To ensure clear communication across the organization, the following operational definitions apply to this document:

* **SDR (Sales Development Representative):** A sales role focused on front-end pipeline activities, primarily outbound cold outreach and qualification of inbound prospects.
* **AE (Account Executive):** A sales role responsible for managing qualified opportunities through discovery, demonstration, negotiation, and contract finalization.
* **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile):** A description of the type of company that would realize the most value from the business’s solutions and provide the highest lifetime value (LTV).
* **MQL (Marketing Qualified Lead):** A lead that has engaged with marketing assets but has not yet been vetted by a sales representative.
* **SQL (Sales Qualified Lead):** A lead that has been qualified by an SDR or AE as meeting baseline budget, authority, need, and timeline requirements.
* **CRM (Customer Relationship Management):** The single source of truth database (e.g., Salesforce, HubSpot) used to track all customer interactions, deals, and sales activities.
* **SOW (Statement of Work):** A formal document defining the scope of work, deliverables, timelines, and costs associated with a client engagement.
* **MSA (Master Services Agreement):** The overarching contract governing the legal relationship between the business and the client.
* **MEDDPICC:** A qualification framework used to evaluate opportunity health, focusing on: **M**etrics, **E**conomic Buyer, **D**ecision Criteria, **D**ecision Process, **P**aper Process, **I**dentify Pain, **C**hampion, and **C**ompetition.

## 4. Roles and Responsibilities

The successful execution of this SOP relies on a clear division of labor across the sales organization. Below are the core roles and their associated responsibilities:

  └────────────────────────┘      └────────────────────────┘      └────────────────────────┘

### The Sales Development Representative (SDR)

* Acts as the primary builder of early-stage pipeline.
* Executes cold outreach and triage of inbound MQLs.
* Secures introductory discovery meetings for the AE.
* Documents initial pain points and technical environments in the CRM.

### The Account Executive (AE)

* Serves as the primary closer of business opportunities.
* Executes deep discovery, demonstrations, proposal creations, and contract negotiations.
* Manages the commercial relationship through to the signature of contracts.
* Coordinates internal deal support (e.g., solutions architects, legal, and product leads).

### The Sales Manager / Director

* Monitors daily, weekly, and monthly pipeline health.
* Performs weekly pipeline reviews with AEs and SDRs to ensure progression.
* Approves custom discounting within designated authority limits.
* Audits CRM data for adherence to this SOP.

### The Customer Success Manager (CSM) / Implementation Lead

* Participates in the late-stage transition phase of the sales process.
* Reviews the sales handoff documentation to verify scope and customer expectations.
* Owns client onboarding immediately following contract execution.

## 5. Prerequisites and Safety Warnings

Before beginning any step in this procedure, all operators must ensure compliance with the following system accesses, legal guidelines, and operational boundaries.

### Required Software and Systems Access

* Active enterprise login to the core CRM system with write permissions for Contacts, Accounts, and Opportunities.
* Provisioned access to the outreach automation platform (e.g., Outreach, Salesloft) linked to email and calendar.
* Prospect research tools (e.g., LinkedIn Sales Navigator, ZoomInfo) with active subscription status.
* Screen-sharing and video conferencing software account synced with calendar invites.
* Proposal generation and electronic signature platform access (e.g., DocuSign, PandaDoc).

### Compliance and Safety Warnings

* **Privacy and Cold Outreach Regulations:** Operators must strictly follow localized privacy laws (GDPR in the EU, CAN-SPAM in the US, POPIA in South Africa). Never send outreach to any contact listed on internal Opt-Out or Do Not Contact registers. Run all cold email lists through validation tools to protect corporate domain reputation.
* **Unauthorized Pricing Promises:** Do not offer pricing discounts, custom contract terms, or SLA guarantees outside of standard product catalogs without written authorization from a Sales Manager or Finance Director. Unauthorized commitments create legal liabilities and damage gross margins.
* **Product Capability Representations:** Do not promise custom features, engineering roadmaps, or unreleased product updates to secure a deal. Selling non-existent features violates our sales ethics and leads to rapid customer churn.

## 6. The Procedure (Step-by-Step Instructions)

This procedure details the transition of a buyer from cold prospect to a signed partner. The process is divided into five distinct operational phases.

### Phase 1: Inbound Lead Triage and Outbound Lead Generation (SDR Role)

#### Step 1.1: Monitor Inbound Lead Queues

Check the CRM inbound lead queue at 08:00, 11:00, 14:00, and 16:30 daily. Inbound contact requests or demo submissions must be claimed and contacted within 15 minutes of system receipt.

#### Step 1.2: Qualify the Inbound Lead Against the ICP

Verify that the lead meets the minimum business requirements before making contact.

* Review company size (minimum 50 employees).
* Review geographic location (must be in active service territories).
* Verify that the prospect's industry does not conflict with our prohibited industries registry.
* *If the lead does not meet the criteria:* Mark the lead status in the CRM as "Disqualified" and select the appropriate reason from the dropdown menu.

#### Step 1.3: Build Target Account Lists for Outbound Prospecting

If inbound lead volume is below target, dedicate 2 hours per day to outbound list building.

* Log into LinkedIn Sales Navigator.
* Apply ICP filters: Industry, Headcount (50-500), and Geography.
* Identify three key target personas per account (typically VP of Operations, Director of Finance, or Lead Architect).
* Import the validated contacts into the CRM under the appropriate target account.

#### Step 1.4: Execute Multi-Channel Outreach Sequences

Add imported prospects to the standard outbound sales cadence. Follow the systematic schedule below:

* **Day 1:** Send personalized email via email automation platform. Send a LinkedIn connection request with no added text.
* **Day 2:** Make a direct phone call. If no answer, do not leave a voicemail.
* **Day 4:** Send a follow-up email threading the original communication. Make a second direct phone call.
* **Day 7:** Send a brief LinkedIn message referencing the threaded email.
* **Day 10:** Send a final "Breakup" email.
* *If contact is made:* Stop the automated sequence immediately and log the notes in the CRM.

#### Step 2.1: Prepare for the Discovery Call

Thirty minutes before the scheduled meeting, review the prospect's profile.

* Review all pre-meeting notes left by the SDR in the CRM.
* Examine the prospect's company website to understand their business model and core offerings.
* Review the prospect's personal LinkedIn profile to understand their tenure and professional focus.

#### Step 2.2: Lead the Structured Discovery Call

Launch the video meeting. Conduct the call using this structured timeline:

* **00:00 - 03:00 (Rapport & Agenda Setup):** State the purpose of the call, confirm the hard stop time, and establish a mutual agenda.
* **03:00 - 15:00 (Pain Investigation):** Ask open-ended questions to uncover business inefficiencies. (e.g., *"What is your current process for managing X?"* or *"How is the current tool impact impacting your team's weekly timeline?"*).
* **15:00 - 22:00 (Value Alignment):** Describe how your solution solves these specific challenges, using similar customer use cases.
* **22:00 - 25:00 (Next Steps):** Confirm the qualification and outline the timeline. Secure a calendar invite for a custom demonstration before concluding the call.

#### Step 2.3: Update and Qualify via the MEDDPICC Framework

Within 1 hour of the call's conclusion, complete the MEDDPICC qualification fields inside the CRM Opportunity Object:

* **Metrics:** Document the economic value of solving the problem (e.g., saving 20 hours per week, reducing overhead by $10,000/month).
* **Identify Pain:** Write down the specific operational bottlenecks the customer described.
* **Economic Buyer:** Note whether the person on the call controls the budget. If not, note who does and your plan to engage them.

### Phase 3: Product Demonstration and Value Alignment (AE Role)

#### Step 3.1: Customize the Demonstration Environment

Never use a generic demo template. Spend 45 minutes tailoring the demo environment to mirror the prospect’s actual workflow.

* Input the prospect’s name, logo, and hypothetical business data into the demonstration environment.
* Arrange the user interface so that the first screen shown directly addresses the "Identify Pain" field documented during the discovery phase.

#### Step 3.2: Execute the Outcome-Oriented Demonstration

Keep the demonstration focused on outcomes rather than individual features.

* Frame every shown capability with a simple structure: **Feature ➔ Operational Benefit ➔ Financial Value**.
* Refrain from detailing features that do not directly address the prospect’s stated pain points.
* Pause every 3 to 4 minutes to confirm alignment. Use check-in questions such as: *"Based on how your team runs things today, how would this impact your current daily workflow?"*

#### Step 3.3: Map the Decision and Paper Process

Before ending the demonstration meeting, clarify the buying process. Ask the client:

* *"What is your typical internal process for evaluating and approving new platforms like ours?"*
* *"Who else on your leadership, IT security, and legal teams needs to review this before we can move forward?"*
* Document these key contacts and the internal steps in the CRM "Decision Process" section.

---

### Phase 4: Proposal Generation, Negotiation, and Closing (AE Role)

#### Step 4.1: Draft the Statement of Work (SOW) and Proposal

Open the corporate proposal generator tool to compile the standard offer.

* Select the approved template matching the client's requested package.
* Insert the custom scope details, implementation timelines, and baseline costs.
* Verify that any applied discount matches the approved table below:

| Discount Tier | Percentage Allowed | Required Approver |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Tier 1** | 0.1% to 10.0% | Account Executive discretion |
| **Tier 2** | 10.1% to 20.0% | Regional Sales Manager sign-off |
| **Tier 3** | 20.1% to 30.0% | VP of Sales signature required |
| **Tier 4** | 30.1%+ | CFO & VP of Sales joint signature required |

* *If custom pricing is required:* Submit the Pricing Exception Request Form in the CRM and secure approval *before* sending the SOW to the client.

#### Step 4.2: Deliver the Proposal via a Guided Live Review

Do not email a proposal directly to a prospect without a meeting scheduled to review it.

* Schedule a brief 15-minute call to review the document together.
* Walk through the deliverables, payment schedules, and legal terms section-by-section.
* Confirm that the client understands the terms before sending the electronic signature link.

#### Step 4.3: Address Pricing and Terms Objections

If the client objects to pricing or terms, use the structured LAER method to handle the feedback:

* **Listen:** Allow the client to explain their objection fully without interrupting.
* **Acknowledge:** Validate their concern (e.g., *"I understand that managing budget allocation is a priority for your team this quarter."*).
* **Explore:** Ask clarifying questions to find the root issue (e.g., *"Is this a concern regarding cash flow and payment timing, or the overall contract value?"*).
* **Respond:** Answer with a structured alternative. Offer flexible payment terms (e.g., shifting from annual upfront to quarterly payments) before discounting the base price.

#### Step 4.4: Issue Final Documents and Monitor Signature Status

Once terms are verbally agreed upon, send the final MSA and SOW through the electronic signature system.

* Set the document expiration date to exactly 14 calendar days from the date of issue.
* Configure automatic reminders to send every 3 days until signed.
* If the document is not signed within 5 business days, place a follow-up call to the main champion to address any unexpected delays.

### Phase 5: Transition to Customer Success and Implementation (AE & CS Roles)

#### Step 5.1: Update Opportunity to Closed-Won

Once both parties have signed the contracts, open the CRM and complete the closing tasks:

* Upload the executed PDF copy of the MSA and SOW directly to the CRM Opportunity record.
* Change the Opportunity Stage to "Closed-Won".
* Fill in the mandatory closing field: **"Reason for Win"** (provide a brief, 3-sentence summary of the core business drivers behind the purchase).

#### Step 5.2: Complete the Internal Sales Handoff Form

Within 24 hours of closing the deal, fill out the handoff document in the CRM to ensure a seamless transition for the customer. Complete the following mandatory fields:

Schedule a 15-minute sync between the closing AE, the assigned CSM, and the Implementation Lead.

* Review the handoff document together.
* Highlight any sensitive dynamics, unique operational needs, or custom expectations established during the negotiation process.
* Confirm that the CS team has the necessary information to proceed with onboarding.

Within 48 hours of closing, send an email to the client introducing the CS team. Use the approved transition template below:

### Troubleshooting and Exception Handling

Refer to the table below for handling common exceptions or unexpected issues that may arise during the sales process:

| Scenario / Issue | Root Cause | Immediate Remedial Action | Prevention Strategy |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Prospect does not attend scheduled Discovery Call** | Lack of reminders; low commitment. | Wait 7 minutes on the line, then exit. Send a polite re-engagement email with an easy rescheduling link. If no response after 3 days, mark the lead as "No-Show." | Configure automated SMS and email reminders 24 hours and 1 hour before the call. |
| **Prospect requests unapproved custom product features** | Product gap; misaligned selling. | State clearly: *"While that feature isn't in our standard offering today, I can submit a feature request to our product team for review."* Do not commit to timelines. | Ensure discovery questions focus on existing capabilities and use cases, rather than open-ended wishlist items. |
| **Prospect demands discounts exceeding standard limits** | Budget constraints; competitive pressure. | Inform the prospect that you need to consult with executive leadership. Submit a pricing exception business case to the Sales Manager for review. | Validate the prospect's budget and cost-of-delay earlier in the process during the discovery phase. |
| **The Executive Sponsor backs out during contract routing** | Lack of engagement; change in budget. | Schedule an immediate call with your champion. Identify the specific objections or concerns raised by the Economic Buyer. | Ensure the Economic Buyer is included in the "Decision Process" and "Decision Criteria" steps prior to Phase 4. |

## 7. Quality Assurance and Metrics

To maintain a consistent, high-performing sales organization, daily adherence to this SOP is monitored through specific quality metrics and regular CRM audits.

### Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)

The effectiveness of this procedure will be evaluated using the following target metrics:

* **Inbound Triage Response Velocity:** Average response time to inbound leads must remain below **15 minutes** during business hours.
* **SOP CRM Compliance Rate:** 100% of Opportunities progressed beyond Stage 2 must have complete MEDDPICC fields filled out in the CRM.
* **Average Proposal Win Rate:** Maintain an average Proposal-to-Closed-Won conversion rate of at least **28%**.
* **Contract Routing Cycle Time:** The average duration from SOW generation to final signature should not exceed **14 calendar days**.
* **Successful CS Handoff Completeness:** 100% of Closed-Won deals must have the Sales Handoff Form completed and an internal CS sync held before the customer kickoff.

### Quality Assurance Auditing Process

* **Weekly Manager Audits:** Every Friday afternoon, Sales Managers will audit five randomly selected active opportunities per rep. Opportunities with missing MEDDPICC fields, outdated close dates, or incomplete action plans will be flagged, and the rep must correct them before Monday.
* **Monthly Pipeline Reviews:** The Sales Operations team will run a monthly performance report. This report tracks conversion velocity between stages to identify process bottlenecks and ensure reps are advancing deals in compliance with this SOP.
